Beat Saber

Beat Saber is a virtual reality rhythm game developed by two Slovaks Ján Ilavský, Vladimír Hrinčár and published by Czech game developer Beat Games. It takes place in many different surrealistic neon environments and features the player slicing blocks representing musical beats with a pair of brightly-colored sabers. Following an early access release in May 2018, the game was officially released for PlayStation 4 and Windows on May 21, 2019, and supports most virtual reality headsets including HTC Vive, Oculus Quest, Quest 2 and Quest Pro, PlayStation VR, PlayStation VR2, and Valve Index.

Spider Man-2

"Spider-Man 2" swings into action as an extraordinary testament to what a superhero game can achieve. From the moment you don the iconic red and blue suit, you're catapulted into the bustling streets of New York City, and the sense of freedom is exhilarating. The web-swinging mechanics are a masterpiece, delivering a genuine feeling of being Spidey as you effortlessly traverse skyscrapers and dive into heart-pounding combat. The open-world design is teeming with life and activities, from thwarting street crimes to pursuing engaging story missions. The narrative is a gripping web of emotion and heroism, and the voice acting is top-notch. The attention to detail in the city's design and the array of iconic Marvel villains you face are nothing short of awe-inspiring. "Spider-Man 2" isn't just a game; it's an immersive journey that encapsulates the essence of the friendly neighborhood superhero, making it an absolute must-play for fans and gamers alike.

Alan Wake II

Alan Wake was heralded as a generational leap in character-driven video games when it launched in 2010, winning over fans and firmly cementing creator Remedy Entertainment as an innovator in gaming. After a couple well-lauded games, the studio is back for Alan Wake 2, and for a few hands-on hours, I got to dive back into the haunted world of a writer and the supernatural darkness he fights. It's our first look ahead of Alan Wake 2's release on Oct. 27, and pricing starts at $49 / £39 for the standard digital edition.

Lords of the Fallen

Lords of the Fallen isn't inspired, but it is a well-sorted game: responsive, playable, balanced, and fair, which in this genre is no easy feat. The combat is precise, fluid, and faster than the original game's, located somewhere between Dark Souls' defensiveness and Bloodborne's aggression.
